Glimpse of Rajasthan Tour

Dig Out the Glorious Past of Rajasthan and Gather Lots of Wonderful Memories

Rajasthan takes pride in its glorious history as hordes and hordes of travellers from every corner of the world come every year to explore its vibrant cultural heritage. Rajput kings had a great passion for art and architecture. Countless forts, palaces and monuments dispersed all over the state testify the same. Today, Rajasthan has become an extremely popular travel destination for travellers of all types and backgrounds. The people of Rajasthan are very friendly and always eager to welcome tourists with open arms. Which is more, the range of traditional Rajasthan cuisines will make anybody's mouth water at the first sight. If you haven't travelled to Rajasthan yet, the Rajasthan Incentive Tour is exactly what you need.

Arrive and explore the numerous ancient and modern-day tourist attractions of Old and New Delhi. Head for Agra which is home to a number of Mughal era buildings including the world-renowned Taj Mahal and the Agra Fort. Next day, leave for Jaipur, the 'Pink City' while visiting Fatehpur Sikri, also known as the 'Ghost Town', en route. When in Jaipur, experience the grandeur of the Amber Fort and soak up the charm of the City Palace. Also visit the Hawa Mahal (meaning the Palace of Winds) and the Jantar Mantar (the Observatory) before flying to Udaipur, famously known as the 'Lake City'. The city of Udaipur is frequented for the very exquisite City Palace and the Lake Pichola along which a boat ride is truly a lot of fun.

Visit the Sahelion Ki Bari, where royal ladies used to take a stroll in the Rajput era, and the Jagdish Temple before travelling back to Delhi for your onward journey or your flight back home.

Day 01 : Arrive Delhi

Upon arrival at Delhi international airport you will be met by the representative of Max Holidays and transferred to your designated hotel for overnight stay.

Day 02 : Delhi sightseeing.

After a lavish breakfast at hotel, enjoy full day splendid guided sightseeing tour of Old Delhi and New Delhi. Visit Red Fort (with 2 kms long red stone wall, built by Mughal Emperor Shahjahan when he moved his capital from Agra to Delhi), Raj Ghat father of nation Mahatma Gandhi is –cremated, while here, we will explore the oldest market on three tyre bicycle rickshaw. Rickshaw walla (bicycle driver) will take us through the narrow streets and busy lanes. Later visit to Jama Masjid one of the largest mosque in India, built by Shahjahan. Stop for a lunch here, and later drive towards New Delhi where we visit Qutab Minar – the highest stone tower built by Qutbuddin Aibak, Humayun Tomb which houses the grave of Bega Begum. Drive through India Gate, which was built in memory of 90,000 Indian soldiers who was died in World War I, along with drive pass through Presidential Palace and Rashtrapati Bhavan (resident of president). After sightseeing return to the hotel for delicious dinner. Overnight stay at Delhi.

Day 03 : Delhi – Agra

After early breakfast at hotel, head towards Agra, a city with the rich history and now a city of Taj Mahal – a monument of love. On arrival, check into hotel and after light refreshments, we proceed to Agra city visiting Agra fort – Made mostly of red sandstone it is strikingly similar to the Red Fort in Delhi. The construction began by Akbar in 1565 AD, which consist of many buildings inside. But most poignant of all is the Musamman Burj, the ornate octagonal tower where Shah Jehan was held captive for eight years, a victim of his son’s Aurangzeb’s imperial ambition. From here, we will drive to Itmad-Ud-Daulah, which was constructed by Noor Jahan in memory of his father Ghias-Ud-Din Beg in 16 Century. During the sunset visit the Taj Mahal built by the Mughul Emperor Shahjahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is said that the Taj Mahal glitters in the light of full moon.

Day 04 : Agra – Fatehpur Sikri – Jaipur

After having breakfast at hotel, head towards Jaipur - Pink City. Enroute, here we will visit Fatehpur Sikri built by the Akhbar as a city of victory, but due to scarcity of water moved to Agra. Inside here, we will visit Diwan-i-Khas (hall to meet ministers, family, friends etc), Panch Mahal (home for royal ladies, from here they can view the local surroundings), Buland Darwaza (made in the success) In the evening, we reach Jaipur and spend rest of the time in comfort at hotel. Evening we visit to the chokhi dhani for a cultural evening. Chokhi Dhani is a artificial village created to provide the feel for local villagers, Rajathani culture and food to the visitors. You can see here the Rajasthani dancers; enjoy camel ride and elephant ride, snake charmers, magic show accompanying the Rajasthani food served in Rajasthani style. Later drive back to hotel for an stay at Jaipur.

Day 05 : Jaipur sightseeing.

After early breakfast, today we head towards Amber Fort, where we will ride on the elephant’s back, which will take us to the entrance of the Fort. This fort is situated atop of Aravali Hills. From here drive back to city, here enroute visit Jal Mahal, located in middle of the Man Sagar Later return to city, where we will have lunch at the local restaurant. After lunch, proceed to visit the other major monuments like Hawa Mahal (the Palaces of Wind) made for the royal ladies, so that they can view the city life from windows, Jantar Mantar – a magnificent astronomical observatory built by Jai Singh II to see the aeronautical events, and City Palace Museum earlier a residence of Maharaja, then later converted into museum by Lt. Swinton Jacob. Night stay in the hotel.

Day 06 : Jaipur to Udaipur (by flight)

After early breakfast transfer to Jaipur airport to board flight to Udaipur also Known as “City of Lakes”. On arrival at Udaipur, our representative will meet you at the airport, assist you to your car and transfer to your designated hotel. Rest of the time is at leisure. In the evening take a walk around city palace area at your own; spend some time in front of lake or hotel restaurants situated on the bank of Pichola Lake.

Day 07 : Udaipur sightseeing.

Today after leisurely breakfast at the hotel, we proceed for one of the beautiful City Palace –

After wash and change, enjoy full day city tour of Udaipur visiting the City Palace & its museum a combination of Rajasthani and Mughal architectural styles set up by Maharana Uday Singh. Next visit the “Sahelion Ki Bari” a garden made for royal ladies for their peaceful leisurely walk. Then visit the Jagdish Temple –the largest and most beautiful temple in this city. At last in evening, enjoy a delightful and relaxing boat ride on Lake Pichola, which takes a stop an island palace – Jag Mandir. Overnight stay is in Udaipur.

Day 08 : Udaipur – Delhi (by flight) Delhi – Departure

After a leisurely breakfast, transfer to Udaipur airport to board flight to Delhi to connect your flight back to home.
